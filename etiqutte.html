Professional Etiquette & Communication

This comprehensive material covers essential aspects of professional etiquette, including social behavior, business protocols, dining etiquette, and telephone communication skills.

Key Takeaways

1. Socialization and Professional Image

  • Socialization is an ongoing process of adapting to social norms and expectations
  • Personal image consists of appearance, body language, communication style, reputation, and presence
  • First impressions form within 30 seconds (judging economic/education level) and 4 minutes (assessing trustworthiness, intelligence, compassion)

2. Business Etiquette Fundamentals

  • Introduce yourself by name rather than title
  • Maintain appropriate eye contact and warm greetings
  • Gracefully accept compliments rather than brushing them off
  • Handle business cards respectfully (receive with both hands, read before storing)
  • Remember names using association techniques
  • Junior employees should stand when seniors enter

3. Dining Etiquette

  • Dining is about connecting with people, not just eating
  • Proper table setting knowledge (bread plates left, drinks right)
  • Continental dining style (fork in left hand, knife in right)
  • Pass food left to right; salt and pepper passed together
  • Napkin placement (lap when seated, chair when temporarily leaving)
  • Avoid discussing politics; maintain cheerful conversation
  • Pace eating with others; don't be first or last to finish
Table Setting Diagram (Bread plates left, drinks right)

4. Telephone Etiquette

  • Answer within 2-3 rings with professional greeting
  • Always ask permission before placing on hold
  • Check back with waiting callers every 30-45 seconds
  • Explain reason before transferring calls
  • Use message forms for accurate documentation
  • Avoid screening calls when possible; be transparent about availability
  • Use positive language instead of negative phrases

5. Professional Conduct

  • Avoid assumptions; ask direct questions when uncertain
  • Give sincere, believable compliments
  • Control body language (avoid fidgeting, maintain confident posture)
  • Dress to convey competence and professionalism
  • Be aware of emotional contagion in social settings
