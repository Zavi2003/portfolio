Professional Skills & Self-Awareness

Developing interpersonal abilities and understanding personal strengths for career success.

Introduction to Professional Skills

This module introduces professional skills as essential non-technical abilities crucial for workplace success. Unlike technical skills, these soft skills are transferable across roles and industries. The course emphasizes continuous assessment through practical assignments including CV preparation, cover letter writing, mock interviews, academic writing, and a self-reflective portfolio.

Key Concepts

Professional/Employability Skills

  • Definition: Interpersonal qualities valued by employers beyond technical expertise
  • Examples: Communication, teamwork, problem-solving, adaptability, time management, leadership
  • Categories:
    • Technical skills (job-specific knowledge)
    • Soft skills (interpersonal abilities)
    • Transferable skills (applicable across contexts)

Johari Window - Self-Awareness Framework

The Johari Window is a model that represents information about a person from four perspectives, helping identify hidden strengths and blind spots through feedback and self-reflection.

Open Area
Known to self and others
Blind Area
Known to others but not self
Hidden Area
Known to self but not others
Unknown Area
Unknown to both self and others

Purpose: Identify hidden strengths and blind spots through feedback and self-reflection to expand the "Open Area" of self-awareness.

Significance

These concepts form the foundation for professional development, emphasizing that technical expertise must be complemented by self-awareness, strong character, and interpersonal skills for career success. The Johari Window provides a structured method for ongoing personal and professional growth.
