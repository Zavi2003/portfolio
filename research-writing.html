Research Writing

Background of the Study

Language policy management in Sri Lanka remains a critical and complex issue, particularly within its multilingual society. The educational sector, especially higher education, is a primary domain where these policies are implemented, with significant implications for academic access, equity, and success.

Research Problem

This study addresses the impact of language policies on student learning within Sri Lankan state universities. Despite established policies, there is a need to examine their practical implementation and consequences on the academic experiences and outcomes of students in a linguistically diverse academic environment.

Research Objectives

The primary objective of this research is to investigate the implementation of language planning and policy within the higher education context, with a specific focus on the Faculty of Management and Finance at the University of Ruhuna.

Literature Review

Existing literature has extensively examined language policies in educational settings, highlighting their profound influence on student learning, identity, and inclusion. Previous studies provide a foundation for understanding the broader theoretical and global context, into which this research aims to insert a specific, localized case study.

Significance of the Study

This research contributes to a deeper understanding of how language policies translate into practice and affect educational outcomes in a multilingual state university setting. The findings are expected to provide valuable insights for policymakers, educators, and administrators aiming to enhance equity and effectiveness in higher education language planning.
